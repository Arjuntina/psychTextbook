About the Authors

Dr. R. H. Ettinger is a professor of psychology at Eastern Oregon University. His research focuses on psychopharmacology and includes investigations of the neural mechanisms underlying feeding and addiction. Notably, his research has led to the development of an anticocaine antibody and the understanding of the role of Pavlovian conditioning in drug tolerance and addiction. In addition to this textbook, Ettinger is the author of a textbook on psychopharmacology. In addition to his research and writing, he is an avid cyclist and cross-country skier, and he enjoys flying his private airplane and providing instrument instruction to other pilots.

Dr. Martha C. Tompson is a professor of psychology at Boston University. Her research focuses on mental health disorders in childhood and adulthood; the impact of family environmental risk factors on adults and children suffering from schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, anxiety and depression; the development and testing of mental health interventions to alter those risk factors and improve outcomes for children and families; and the integration of evidence-based interventions for youth into community pediatric settings. Dr. Tompson has published widely, is associate editor for a leading professional journal, and continues to see patients and their families in therapy. In her spare time she enjoys cycling, kayaking, and listening to music.
